How to divide roles in a 3-member ICPC team?

ICPC is not just about having three good coders. It is about building a team where each person brings a different strength. A strong team usually needs one person who is good at fast implementation, one who is strong in algorithms and observations, and one who is calm under pressure and good at debugging/testing.

A simple team structure can look like this:

Member 1: Fast Coder  
Strong in implementation, templates, STL, graphs, DP coding, and converting ideas into code quickly.

Member 2: Problem Solver  
Strong in observations, proofs, greedy, DP, math, and finding the correct approach.

Member 3: Debugger + Strategist  
Strong in testing, edge cases, reading all problems, choosing which problem to solve first, and managing penalty time.

But roles should not be fixed forever. In a real contest, roles change depending on the problem. If the problem is math-heavy, the math person leads. If the problem is implementation-heavy, the fast coder leads. If the team is stuck, the strategist should decide when to switch problems.

How to pick teammates from your college?

Do not choose only your closest friends. Choose people based on consistency, communication, and complementary strengths. One person may be very strong in DP, another in graphs, and another in implementation. The best ICPC team is not three identical coders; it is three people who cover each other’s weaknesses.

Before finalizing a team, give a few mock contests together. Check these things:

Can we communicate without ego?  
Can we accept mistakes quickly?  
Can we debug together?  
Can we divide work under pressure?  
Can we stay calm after wrong submissions?

In ICPC, teamwork is an algorithm too.

The right team can convert average individual skill into strong contest performance.
